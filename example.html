 Queues are ideal for processing tasks in the order they are received, adhering to a first-in, first-out (FIFO) principle. A great example is a customer support chat system, where incoming chat requests are added to a queue and served in the order they were received. O(1) (Enqueue at rear) O(1) (Dequeue from front)

Maps are used to store and retrieve data in key-value pairs. They provide highly efficient lookups by using a unique key to access an associated value. A practical example is a contact list app where a map stores contact names (keys) and their phone numbers (values). O(1) O(1) (by key)

 Balanced Binary Search Trees (BSTs) are used for efficiently managing sorted data. They automatically balance themselves to ensure that search, insertion, and deletion operations maintain a logarithmic time complexity. A flight booking system could use a BST to store available seats by seat number, allowing for quick searches. O(log n) O(log n)
